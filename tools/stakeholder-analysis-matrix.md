| Name/Role | Power | Influence | Current Attitude | Target Attitude | Management Strategy | Owner |
|-----------|------|--------|---------|---------|---------|--------|
| [CEO] | High | High | Supportive | Maintain Supportive | Monthly 1-on-1 Briefing | CIO |
| [CFO] | High | Medium | Neutral | Supportive | One-on-one ROI communication, proactively address financial concerns | CIO+PM |
| [Business VP-X] | Medium | High | Resistant | Neutral | Early involvement in design + Listen to specific concerns + Pilot to benefit their team first | PM |

| [Frontline Employee Group] | Low | High | Unknown | Supportive | Change champion network + Frequent transparent communication | Change Lead |

### Step 4: Develop Strategy

| Matrix Position | Management Strategy |
|---------|---------|
| **Key Players (High Power + High Influence)** | Manage Closely: Face-to-face 1-on-1, deep involvement in decisions, personalized communication |
| **Keep Satisfied (High Power + Low Influence)** | Keep Satisfied: Regular executive briefings, don't let them hear bad news from others |
| **Keep Informed (Low Power + High Influence)** | Keep Informed: Regular emails/briefings, make them feel respected and informed |
| **Minimal Effort (Low Power + Low Influence)** | Minimal Effort: Routine project communications, don't neglect but don't over-invest |

## Tool 3: Key Influencer Network Map

Identify the "who influences whom" relationship network for targeted communication:

```
Key Person Identification:
1. Who is the "opinion leader" — when they say good, a group says good; when they say bad, a group says bad?
   Name: [____]  Usually not the highest-ranking, but the most influential

2. Who is the "information hub" — people habitually get information from them?
   Name: [____]  If the information they get is wrong, the entire team's information will be wrong

3. Who is the "CEO's ear" — who does the CEO consult before making decisions?
   Name: [____]  If this person is not supportive, the CEO is very likely also not supportive

4. Who has been around the longest — experienced the most "they said this last time too but didn't deliver"?
   Name: [____]  They can persuade for or kill a change
```

**Relationship Map** (Hand-drawn or Miro):
```
        [CEO]────[CFO]          ← C-Suite
          │
    ┌─────┼─────┐
    │     │     │
  [VP1] [VP2] [VP3]            ← Business Lines
    │     │
    ├──[Director A]  ← Opinion Leader (Their attitude influences VP1+VP2)
    │
  [Team B] [Team C] [Team D]   ← Frontline
```

## Usage Scenarios

- **At Project Initiation**: Use Tool 1+3 to build initial stakeholder map
- **During Project Execution**: Use Tool 2+5 to track changes, alert on people whose "attitude is deteriorating"
- **Before Key Decisions**: Use Tool 4 to develop personalized communication strategy
- **When Facing Resistance**: Use Tool 3 to identify who can influence whom

---

## Key Principles

1. **Stakeholder analysis is not a one-time exercise** — people change, attitudes change, update monthly
2. **"Resistant" people are often the most important signals** — listening carefully to why they resist provides critical insights
3. **Don't only focus on people who support you** — ignoring resistant people, they may organize greater opposition behind your back
4. **The CEO is not necessarily the most important** — sometimes a trusted middle manager can drive change more effectively than the CEO
5. **Attitudes are contagious** — one person shifting from Supportive to Resistant will take everyone they influence with them
