# 03 — Proposal Development

## Objectives
Transform the preliminary diagnosis and understanding into a formal proposal that wins the project. The core task of a proposal is to answer three questions in the client's mind: "Do you understand my problem? Is your approach feasible? Why choose you?"

## Prerequisites
- At least 1-2 in-depth client communications completed
- Problem diagnosis summary produced
- Client budget range and decision-making process understood
- Competitors understood (if disclosed by the client)

## Inputs
- Problem diagnosis summary
- Client meeting minutes
- Client-provided RFP (if any)
- Industry benchmark data

---

## Operation Steps

### Step 1: Define Proposal Strategy — Three Things to Clarify Before Writing

**1. Who is the real decision maker?**

```
Decision Maker Type    What They Care About                       How to Pitch the Proposal
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
CEO/Business Sponsor   Business outcomes, competitive advantage, stock price/valuation    Executive Summary + ROI + Risk
CIO/CTO                Technical feasibility, team capability, long-term architecture     Methodology + Team + Technical Depth
CFO/Procurement        Total price, cost-effectiveness, payment terms, vendor risk        TCO + Benchmark Comparison + Payment Plan
Business VP            Benefits to their own department, impact on daily work             Business Scenarios + Change Management
```

**2. What is the client's budget tier?**
- >$1M: Full proposal + on-site presentation required
- $200K-$1M: Streamlined proposal + 1-2 in-depth discussions
- <$200K: 3-5 page solution summary + quotation

**3. Is this project a "vitamin" or a "painkiller" for the client?**
- Painkiller (compliance requirements/security incidents/CEO direct mandate): Emphasize speed + certainty + risk control
- Vitamin (efficiency improvement/innovation/competitiveness): Emphasize ROI + industry trends + benchmark cases

### Step 2: Standard Proposal Structure

```
Cover Page
  ├── Project Name
  ├── Client Company Logo + Client Name
  ├── Your Company Logo
  ├── Date
  └── Confidentiality Level (Confidential)

Chapter 1: Executive Summary — 1 page
Chapter 2: Our Understanding of the Client's Situation — 1-2 pages
Chapter 3: Our Approach & Methodology — 2-4 pages
Chapter 4: Project Scope & Deliverables — 1-2 pages
Chapter 5: Our Team — 1-2 pages
Chapter 6: Timeline — 1 page
Chapter 7: Investment & Commercials — 1-2 pages
Chapter 8: Why Us — 1 page
Chapter 9: Next Steps — 0.5 page
Appendices
```

### Step 3: Chapter-by-Chapter Writing Guide

**Chapter 1: Executive Summary — The Most Important Page**

Bad:
> "XX Company is a leading XX enterprise facing the challenges of digital transformation. We recommend conducting IT strategic planning..."

Good:
> "XX Company's core ERP system has been running for 12 years, with an average requirement response time of 4 months, compared to 2 weeks for industry leaders. Without action, an estimated $15-20M in market share will be lost over the next 3 years due to lagging speed-to-market. We recommend a phased modernization approach, investing $3.2M, reducing requirement response time to 4 weeks within 12 months, and to 1 week within 24 months. Compared to the 'do nothing' scenario, the 5-year net benefit is no less than $12M."

Core Formula: **Problem (quantified pain point) → Solution (clear direction) → Impact (measurable benefit) → Credibility (why us)**

**Chapter 2: Our Understanding of the Client's Situation**

This is the key chapter that demonstrates "you understand me." Structure:

```
2.1 Industry Trends & Challenges
  [3-5 key trends in the client's industry, 1-2 sentences each + impact on the client]

2.2 Client's Business Strategy & IT Implications
  Business Strategy            →          IT Implication
  "Enter 5 new markets in 3 years"   →     Requires rapidly replicable infrastructure and core systems
  "Growth through M&A"               →     Requires flexible integration architecture and pluggable systems

2.3 Core Challenges We Understand
  [3-5 core challenges, each: symptom → business impact → consequences if not addressed]

2.4 Our Dialogue Journey with the Client
  [Briefly describe the interactions so far, demonstrating deep engagement]
```

**Chapter 3: Our Approach & Methodology**

This is the chapter that demonstrates methodological rigor:

```
3.1 Overall Methodology Framework
  [1 diagram showing the overall approach: Phase 1→2→3→4]

3.2 Specific Activities, Outputs, and Timeline for Each Phase
  Phase 1: [Name & Objective]
    Key Activities: [5-8 items]
    Deliverables: [List]
    Client Participation Required: [List, manage expectations]
    Duration: [_] weeks

  Phase 2: [Same structure]
  ...

3.3 Methodology Differentiation from Similar Projects
  [1-2 differentiating points, not disparaging others, but showcasing unique thinking]
  e.g.: "Most consulting firms build the technology platform first in AI transformation; we insist on starting with 3-5 use cases with quantifiable ROI"
```

**Chapter 4: Project Scope & Deliverables**

```
In Scope:
  ├── [Deliverable 1: Name + Brief Description]
  ├── [Deliverable 2: Name + Brief Description]
  └── [...]

Out of Scope (explicitly excluded, to prevent scope creep):
  ├── [Excluded item 1 + Brief Reason]
  ├── [Excluded item 2 + Brief Reason]
  └── [...]

Assumptions (if these assumptions do not hold, scope/timeline/fees may need adjustment):
  ├── Key client personnel commit no less than X days/week during the project
  ├── Client provides X data within 2 weeks of project kickoff
  ├── Client IT team cooperates to enable technical environment access
  └── [...]
```

### Step 4: Pricing Strategy

**Four Pricing Models:**

| Model | Applicable Scenario | Client Perception | Risk Ownership |
|---------|---------|---------|---------|
| Fixed Price | Clear scope, low uncertainty | Predictable budget | We bear overrun risk |
| Time & Materials (T&M) | Fuzzy scope, exploratory | Pay for actual effort | Client bears uncertainty |
| Value-Based | Clearly quantifiable benefits | Tied to outcomes | Shared risk & reward |
| Monthly Retainer | Long-term ongoing support | Ongoing relationship | Shared |

**Pricing Recommendations:**
- If scope is uncertain: Fixed Price (Phase 1) + T&M (Phase 2+)
- Base rates: Partner $400-600/h, Director $300-400/h, Manager $250-350/h, Consultant $200-280/h, Analyst $150-200/h
- Above are reference rates for global tier-1 consulting firms; adjust based on region and market

**Common Pricing Mistakes:**
- Pricing too low to win the project → Only junior staff can be assigned, quality drops, reputation suffers
- Only giving a total price without breakdown → Client can't compare horizontally, easy to negotiate down
- No exit mechanism → Both sides awkward when the project can't continue

## Outputs
1. **Decision Maker Profile**: Understand the decision chain and each person's concerns
2. **Proposal**: Complete 9-chapter proposal document
3. **Pricing Model**: Choose among 4 pricing models + pricing rationale
4. **Client Follow-up Plan**: Follow-up actions after proposal delivery

## Quality Checklist

- [ ] Executive summary can be read independently and conveys core message (within 1 page)
- [ ] "Understanding of Client's Situation" chapter makes the client say "Yes, that's exactly our problem"
- [ ] Methodology has logic, not a patchwork of frameworks
- [ ] Out of Scope section is as detailed as In Scope
- [ ] Team member experience is highly relevant to project requirements
- [ ] Timeline accounts for client-side commitment and decision cycles
- [ ] Pricing has a rationale explanation (not just bare numbers)
- [ ] Why Us section is not empty claims ("we have experience") but specific matching
- [ ] Total proposal pages do not exceed 25 (excluding appendices)

## Common Pitfalls

| Pitfall | Correct Approach |
|---------|------------------|
| Proposal = Pile of methodology PPT slides | Clients buy outcomes, not methodology. Methodology builds trust; outcomes are the purchase reason |
| "We are the best" empty claims | Use specific cases instead of adjectives: "In Project XX we helped the client achieve X" > "We have rich experience" |
| Team roster is all partners, actual delivery is all analysts | Honestly note each person's allocation percentage and specific role |
| Not mentioning competitors (pretending they don't exist) | At least hint that you know other options: "Compared to technology implementation alone, our strategy + delivery closed loop is unique value" |
| Sending the proposal and waiting for a response | Proactively contact within 24 hours of sending: "Is there anything I can further explain?" |
