## Case C01: Global Supply Chain Digital Transformation Strategy Planning

> **Real Case Source**: Dell Technologies global supply chain digital transformation, sourced from the public case study by MIT CTL Digital Supply Chain Transformation Lab (Sáenz, Borrella & Revilla), Ivey Publishing's best-selling operations management case of 2024/2025.

### Project Background

**Client**: A global tech manufacturing enterprise with annual revenue exceeding $80 billion, supply chain covering 50+ countries, 300+ suppliers, and 20+ manufacturing bases (*Note: Client profile is abstracted from Dell Technologies public materials; specific interview content is inferred based on industry practice.*)

**Trigger Event**: Exponential growth in global supply chain complexity — SKU count tripled, order fragmentation, frequent geopolitical disruptions — traditional manual decision-making models can no longer respond to E2E collaboration demands. The supply chain planning team spends 60%+ of their time daily on data collection and manual coordination.

**Project Objective**: Design and implement an AI-driven E2E supply chain orchestration system, achieving the leap from "manual decision-making → automated execution → intelligent orchestration."

### Phase 0: Business Development and Contract Signing

**Initial Communication Minutes (SCQA Framework)**:

| SCQA | Content |
|------|------|
| **S (Situation)** | Global supply chain operations at massive scale — annual procurement in the tens of billions of dollars, but significant disconnects exist between supply chain planning and execution, with an average 48-hour delay from planning data to on-site execution |
| **C (Complication)** | Frequent shortages of critical components (e.g., hard disk drives), quarterly stockout losses exceeding tens of millions of dollars; aging inventory ratio continues to climb, tying up working capital; competitors have already deployed AI scheduling and digital twins |
| **Q (Question)** | How to build AI-driven E2E supply chain orchestration capabilities, achieving a paradigm shift from "reactive response" to "autonomous decision-making" within 3 years? |
| **A (Answer)** | Three-phase roadmap: first "visibility" (E2E data integration and visualization), then "accuracy" (AI forecasting and intelligent scheduling), finally "agility" (autonomous orchestration and closed-loop self-healing) |

**Core Chapters of the Project Proposal** (9-chapter standard structure):

1. Project Understanding: When supply chain complexity exceeds the limits of human processing — 50+ countries globally, 300+ suppliers, millions of SKU combinations
2. Methodology: MECE Five-Dimensional Diagnosis + AIM² Maturity Model + MIT DSCT (Digital Supply Chain Transformation) Framework
3. Scope of Work: Current state diagnosis → Capability blueprint design → AI orchestration platform architecture → Roadmap planning → Quick win verification
4. Team Composition: 1 Project Director + 2 Supply Chain Architects + 2 AI/ML Experts + 1 Change Management Expert + 2 Data Engineers
5. Timeline: 16 weeks (Diagnosis 4 weeks + Blueprint Design 4 weeks + Platform Architecture 3 weeks + Roadmap 2 weeks + Quick win POC 3 weeks)
6. Deliverables: Diagnostic Report + DSCT Capability Blueprint + AI Orchestration Platform Architecture Design + 3-Year Roadmap + Quick win POC Report
7. Fee: 5.2 million (including travel and POC prototype development)
8. Team Credentials: Core members of MIT CTL Supply Chain Digitalization Research Program, having served 3 Fortune 500 tech manufacturing enterprises
9. Next Steps: Initiate diagnosis within 2 weeks after signing

### Phase 1: Project Kickoff and Diagnosis

**IT/Supply Chain Current State Five-Dimensional Diagnosis (MECE)**:

| Dimension | Current Rating | Key Findings |
|------|:---:|------|
| **Technology** | 2/5 | 30+ legacy systems, severe data silos, no unified data lake, AI/ML infrastructure at pilot stage |
| **Process** | 2/5 | Demand forecasting relies on Excel + experience, supplier collaboration via email/phone, exception handling requires cross-department coordination across 6 departments on average |
| **Organization** | 2/5 | Planning/Procurement/Logistics/Manufacturing operate in silos, no E2E supply chain visibility role, data science team only 5 people |
| **Data** | 1/5 | Poor master data quality (material master data accuracy only 78%), supplier data missing rate 40%, no unified data governance |
| **Finance** | 2/5 | Annual inventory holding costs exceed $2 billion, annual stockout losses ~$300 million, supply chain accounts for only 12% of IT budget |

**AIM² AI Maturity Assessment**:

| Level | Description | Current Status | Target (3 Years) |
|:---:|------|:---:|:---:|
| L1 Initial | Rule-driven, no AI capability | | |
| L2 Exploratory | Pilot AI forecasting, anomaly detection | ✅ Current | Completed |
| L3 Scaled | AI embedded in E2E orchestration core processes | | End of Year 1 |
| L4 Optimized | AI autonomous decision-making + human-machine collaboration | | End of Year 2 |
| L5 Leading | AI Native supply chain, self-healing closed loop | | End of Year 3 |

**Stakeholder Interviews (Four-Wave Approach)** (*Note: The following interview content is inferred based on industry practice.*):

| Wave | Target | Key Findings |
|:---:|------|----------|
| Wave 1 | SVP Global Supply Chain / CFO | "Supply chain complexity grows 20% annually, but our decision-making speed hasn't changed. We need AI to automate 5,000 daily decisions, letting people focus on 50 strategic decisions." |

### Phase 2: Strategy and Solution Design

**Supply Chain Digital Transformation Strategy (Run/Grow/Transform Model)**:

| Category | Budget Share | Core Projects | Expected Value |
|------|:---:|------|------|
| **Run** (Operations) | 30% | Unified data foundation (Data Lakehouse), master data governance, API integration gateway | Data accessibility from 48 hours → real-time, master data accuracy from 78% → 95% |
| **Grow** (Growth) | 40% | AI demand forecasting, intelligent inventory optimization, supplier risk early warning | Forecast accuracy improvement of 5-15%, aging inventory reduction of 50% |
| **Transform** (Transformation) | 30% | E2E AI orchestration engine, digital twin sandbox, autonomous decision-making agents | 5,000+ actions automated per quarter, decision speed 2x, productivity improvement 40%+ |

**3-Year Roadmap**:

| Year | Theme | Core Projects | Key Milestones |
|:---:|------|------|------|
| Year 1 | Data Foundation + AI Forecasting | Unified data foundation construction, master data governance, AI demand forecasting model deployment, supplier risk radar | Q2 Data foundation go-live, Q4 AI forecasting model covers 80% of SKUs |
| Year 2 | E2E Orchestration + Intelligent Inventory | AI orchestration engine V1 (Plan → Procure → Manufacture → Logistics), intelligent inventory optimization, HDD supply chain special initiative | Q2 Orchestration engine covers 3 core product lines, Q4 HDD component shortage reduced by 77% |
| Year 3 | Autonomous Decision-Making + Digital Twin | Orchestration engine V2 (autonomous decision-making + human-machine collaboration), digital twin sandbox, global rollout | Q2 Autonomous decision-making covers 60% of routine scenarios, Q4 global rollout across all regions completed |

**Quick Win Projects**:

| E2E Visualization Dashboard | 1 million | 2 months | Replace manual daily reports, real-time supply chain status visibility |

### Phase 5: Change Management

**ADKAR Change Model Application**:

| Phase | Action |
|------|------|
| **Awareness** | Global Supply Chain SVP all-staff letter + "AI is not replacing you, it's freeing you from 5,000 daily decisions" vision communication |
| **Desire** | Quick win projects let the planning team experience value firsthand — HDD shortage early warning transforms the team from "firefighting" to "fire prevention" |
| **Knowledge** | Three-tier training system: Executive AI strategy workshop, Planner AI collaboration operational training, Data team MLOps technical training |
| **Ability** | Establish "Supply Chain AI Orchestration Operations Center," assign 1 AI Supply Chain BP (Business Partner) per region |
| **Reinforcement** | Monthly automated action volume dashboard, quarterly E2E KPI review (forecast accuracy, inventory health, decision latency) |

### Lessons Learned

1. **AI orchestration is not a technology project, it's an operating model transformation**: The Dell case proves that the real value lies not in the algorithm itself, but in embedding AI into the E2E decision-making closed loop
2. **Quick wins must target the "pain of pains"**: HDD shortage was the "Achilles' heel" of Dell's supply chain — the 77% improvement not only saved money but also won organizational confidence in AI transformation
3. **Five-dimensional diagnosis reveals root causes**: Behind the technology score of 2/5 lies organization at 2/5 and data at 1/5 — cure data and organization first, then technology can deliver value
4. **Run/Grow/Transform gives budget allocation strategic logic**: The 30/40/30 split both safeguards daily operations from disruption and reserves ample ammunition for transformation
5. **AIM² progressive roadmap dispels "AI omnipotence" thinking**: Each stage from L2→L3→L4 has quantifiable KPIs, allowing the board to see the return on investment at every step

## Case C02: Agentic AI Enterprise Software Business Model Due Diligence

> **Real Case Source**: Mininglamp Technology (2718.HK) 2025 annual financial report and public reports. Mininglamp Technology was listed on the HKEX Main Board on November 3, 2025, and is known as "the world's first Agentic AI stock." Client names and interview content are inferred based on industry practice.

### Project Background

**Client**: A leading TMT PE fund (AUM exceeding 50 billion RMB), planning a Pre-IPO round investment decision for a Chinese AI enterprise software company (*Note: Target company profile is abstracted from Mininglamp Technology public materials.*)

**Due Diligence Objective**: Evaluate the target company's technical capabilities (Agentic AI technology stack), business model innovation (RaaS vs SaaS), financial health, organizational efficiency, and competitive moat

**Timeline**: Complete technical and business due diligence report within 4 weeks

### Four-Dimensional Due Diligence Framework

#### Dimension 1: Technology & AI Capability Assessment

**AI Technology Stack Assessment**:

| Assessment Item | Findings | Rating |
|--------|------|:---:|
| Proprietary Model Capability | Self-developed Cito reasoning model (BFCL small model leaderboard #1), Mano GUI-VLA model (OS-World leaderboard #2, Mind2Web leaderboard #1), 13 benchmarks SOTA | ✅ Leading |
| Agent Platform | Self-developed multi-agent open-source collaboration platform Octo, 2,900+ agents deployed internally, 1,400 employees using | ✅ Leading |
| Enterprise AI Foundation | DeepMiner platform — "Orchestration-Decision-Execution" multi-agent layered architecture, full-chain AI auto-completion rate 90% | ✅ Good |
| Data Assets | Miaozhen Systems covers 90%+ of China's internet advertising traffic monitoring, nearly 20 years of data accumulation | ✅ Leading |
| Open Source Strategy | Mano-P calling skills and on-device models open-sourced, Octo platform open-sourced | ✅ Good |
| AI Native Hardware | First AI Native hardware Octic (meeting scenario), signaling software-hardware integration extension | ⚠️ Early Stage |
| Patent Portfolio | 2,400+ patents | ✅ Leading |

**AI Capability Score**: 8.5/10

#### Dimension 2: Business Model & Financial Quality

**Business Model Analysis — From SaaS to RaaS**:

| Assessment Item | Findings | Rating |
|--------|------|:---:|
| Core Pricing Model | From traditional SaaS (per-seat/functional module pricing) → RaaS/Agentic Services (pricing based on quantifiable business outcomes) | ✅ Innovative |
| Agentic Services Revenue | First year (2025) revenue exceeded 100 million RMB, accounting for 7% of total revenue, 30%+ of new major clients from this segment | ✅ High Growth |
| Gross Margin | 55.4%, up 3.8pct YoY, reflecting systematic compression of delivery costs by AI | ✅ Improving |
| Key Client Renewal Rate | 96%, stable core client base | ✅ Excellent |
| Total Revenue | 1.426 billion RMB, +3.2% YoY (modest on surface, but significant structural change) | ⚠️ Moderate Growth |
| Adjusted Net Profit | 42.04 million RMB, turning profitable (vs. -45.11 million RMB in same period 2024) | ✅ Inflection Point |
| R&D Expense Trend | 2022: 751M → 2024: 353M, down 53%, but code output grew 10x | ✅ Efficiency Revolution |
| Client Base | 135 Fortune Global 500, ~2,100 brand clients, 240,000+ enterprise users | ✅ Deep |

**Key Structural Signals**:

| Metric | Traditional SaaS Expectation | Mininglamp Actual Performance | Signal Interpretation |
|------|:---:|:---:|------|
| R&D expense down 53% | Declining competitiveness | Code output grew 10x | Systematic restructuring of R&D efficiency by AI |
| 5-person team output | ~50K lines/year | 370K lines of effective code | Equivalent to 50-80 people over 18 months of traditional work |
| Gross margin growth vs. revenue growth | Synchronized or slightly higher | 10.8% vs. 3.2% (3.4x) | High-margin business share rapidly increasing |

**Business Model Score**: 8/10

#### Dimension 3: Organizational Efficiency & AI Native Operations

**AI's Restructuring of Organizational Efficiency** (Based on public financial report data):

| Dimension | Key Data | Implication |
|------|------|------|
| All-staff AI access | 100% access to DeepMiner platform | AI is not just a tool department matter, it's the organizational operating system |
| Marketing intelligence delivery efficiency | Up to 4x improvement | Same headcount, 4x output |
| Operational intelligence work orders | Resolution time compressed by 30%+ | Customer service costs systematically declining |
| Miaozhen Systems Agentic transformation | 60-day full-chain integration, AI auto-completion rate 90% | Deep-dive report per-capita efficiency improved 20x |
| R&D "scissors gap" | R&D expense -53%, code output +10× | AI Native R&D paradigm verified |

**Organizational Efficiency Score**: 8.5/10

#### Dimension 4: Competitive Moat & Risks

**Competitive Landscape Assessment**:

| Dimension | Assessment | Risk Level |
|------|------|:---:|
| Industry Data Moat | Nearly 20 years of marketing data accumulation, covering 90%+ internet advertising traffic, extremely high migration cost | 🟢 Low Risk |
| Client Stickiness | 96% key client renewal rate, some major clients cooperating for 10+ years, RaaS model deepens dependency | 🟢 Low Risk |
| Giant Competition | Huawei, Alibaba, Tencent all have Agentic AI deployments, but Mininglamp's vertical industry know-how + data is a differentiated moat | 🟡 Medium Risk |
| RaaS Scaling Risk | Model prediction accuracy must be continuously maintained, client industry diversification may bring marginal cost volatility | 🟡 Medium Risk |
| Technology Path Risk | Paradigm migration from data intelligence → Agentic AI, must continuously maintain model leadership | 🟡 Medium Risk |
| Ecosystem Dependency | Tencent holds ~27% stake, both a resource and a dependency; Tencent's own advertising business expansion may create co-opetition tension | 🟡 Medium Risk |
| Free Float Risk | Newly listed stock, extremely low actual free float in initial period (lock-up period), high share price volatility | 🟡 Medium Risk |

**Competitive Moat Score**: 7.5/10

### Technical/Business Debt Quantification

| Debt/Risk Type | Quantitative Estimate | Business Impact | Priority |
|----------|:---:|------|:---:|
| RaaS model accuracy sustained investment | ~50M RMB/year | Model prediction errors borne by service provider, gross margin may face pressure | P0 |
| Giant competition response (Huawei/Alibaba/Tencent) | ~100M RMB/year (ecosystem building + open-source community) | Differentiated moat may be eroded by general-purpose large models | P0 |
| Client concentration risk | Top 5 client dependency needs continuous monitoring | If major clients develop in-house alternatives, significant revenue impact | P1 |
| Agentic Services scaling verification | Current 100M RMB revenue base is small, need to verify cross-industry replicability | Core assumption of the growth story | P1 |
| Software-hardware integration (Octic) investment | Estimated 50M+ RMB | Hardware is a completely new domain, execution risk exists | P2 |
| **Total** | **~200M RMB/year** | | |

### Due Diligence Conclusion

**Overall Score**: 8.1/10

**Investment Recommendation**: Recommend investment, recommend stipulating in the investment agreement:

1. **Agentic Services Revenue Target**: Agentic Services revenue share to increase to 20%+ within 2 years post-closing (currently 7%)
2. **Gross Margin Threshold**: Maintain 55%+ gross margin level, verifying RaaS model scalability
3. **Key Client Renewal Rate**: Maintain 95%+ renewal rate as the core KPI for client value verification
4. **R&D Efficiency Continuous Tracking**: Code output / R&D expense ratio to continue improving, verifying AI Native R&D paradigm
5. **Valuation Logic**: Adopt sum-of-the-parts valuation: SaaS (P/S) + Agentic Services (premium multiple based on GMV/outcome-based pricing)

### Lessons Learned

1. **Business model innovation is harder to judge than technological innovation**: The SaaS→RaaS switch is not a product upgrade, it's a redefinition of pricing power. The key is verifying whether gross margin can be maintained under the "outcome-based pricing" model
2. **A big drop in R&D expense is not necessarily bad — look at code output**: Mininglamp's 53% R&D expense decline + 10x code output growth "scissors gap" is the core signal of the AI Native R&D paradigm
3. **96% renewal rate is the most honest client vote**: Against the backdrop of pressured renewal rates in the SaaS industry, this number shows clients are getting quantifiable business results from the RaaS model
4. **5 people producing 370K lines of code is a "dimensional strike" against traditional software engineering**: When AI compresses marginal delivery costs to 1/5, the cost advantage of traditional SaaS vendors instantly collapses
5. **Sum-of-the-parts valuation responds to business model transition**: SaaS base with P/S, Agentic Services with outcome-based pricing premium, avoiding misjudgment from a single valuation framework
